Risk ID: R1
Paper ID: P01/P02
Risk Name: Incomplete or Erroneous Data Migration
Risk Description: Errors occurring during the transfer of data from old (often non-relational) databases to modern systems, leading to data corruption or system disturbance.
Modernization Strategy: Migration / Replacement
Dimension: Technical
Granularity Level: Component-level (Database)
Root Cause / Challenge: Use of obsolete database models that do not support modern relational structures.
Trigger Event: Execution of the cut-over or data ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) phase.
Impact / Consequences: New system instability, loss of business-critical data, high regaining costs.
Linked Risks: System Downtime, Business Disruption.
Stakeholders: Database Administrator, Software Architect, Data Analyst
Contextual Constraints: Old non-supported databases, big data
Evidence Type: Case Study / Expert Opinion
Detection Source: Database Scheme Audit
Key Terminology (Tags): #LegacyModernization, #DataMigration
Standardized Risk Entry: Data migration is a critical risk factor where errors in transferring data from obsolete structures to modern environments can lead to system instability and business disruption.
Key Quote for Prompting: "The main risk in modernization is that the data migration, which cannot be done perfectly. Errors are made and you have some risk that your new system is disturbed."
Logic Pattern: IF [Non-Relational Database] AND [High Business Criticality] THEN [Incomplete or Erroneous Data Migration]


Risk ID: R2
Paper ID: P01, P04, G01
Risk Name: Scarcity of Experts and Documentation
Risk Description: Loss of knowledge as original developers retire or leave, combined with outdated or missing system documentation.
Modernization Strategy: Re-engineering / Migration
Dimension: Organizational/Process
Granularity Level: System-wide
Root Cause / Challenge: Aging workforce and lack of continuous documentation updates during the system's lifecycle
Trigger Event: Retirement of key personnel
Impact / Consequences: Project delays, inability to understand business logic, increased risk of system failure during modernization.
Linked Risks: Incomplete or Erroneous Data Migration, Time Overrun
Stakeholders: IT Manager, HR, Lead Architect
Contextual Constraints: Leaving of a former architect
Evidence Type: Statistical / Expert Opinion
Detection Source: Documentation Audit
Key Terminology (Tags): #ExpertTurnover, #UndocumentedSystem, #KnowledgeLoss, #TechnicalDebt
Standardized Risk Entry: The loss of institutional knowledge and lack of up-to-date documentation regarding legacy system internals significantly increase the risk of errors and project failure during modernization
Key Quote for Prompting: "The big problem is that you can't find people to understand them (legacy systems) and understand the technology"
Logic Pattern: IF [Original Developers Absent] AND [Documentation Outdated] THEN [Scarcity of Experts and Documentation]

Risk Name: Time Overrun

Modernization Strategy:

Risk ID: R4
Paper ID: P01, P04, G01, P06
Risk Name: Cost Overrun
Risk Description: Initial cost estimations for migration (finance, time, personnel) are significantly lower than actual requirements.
Modernization Strategy: Redeveloping / Refactoring
Dimension: Business
Granularity Level: Enterprise-wide
Root Cause / Challenge: Underestimating complexity and hidden costs (e.g., service wrapping vs. subsequent replacement).
Trigger Event: Lack of insight into required migration budget and legacy system complexity.
Impact / Consequences: Financial waste; project delays; potential project cancellation. In one case, costs were 500% over budget.
Linked Risks: Resource Shortage
Stakeholders: Project Manager, Financial Department
Contextual Constraints: Especially high risk when "service wrapping" hides the need for future deep code replacement.
Evidence Type: Case Study (Swedish Company / European Bank)
Detection Source: Initial Budget vs. Actual Spend
Key Terminology (Tags): #Budgeting #CostOverrun #FinancialRisk
Standardized Risk Entry: Underestimating modernization costs due to legacy complexity can lead to massive budget overruns and wasted resources.
Key Quote for Prompting: "The real cost and time were around 500% more than the initial estimated budget."
Logic Pattern: IF [Lack of insight on required budget] AND [Migration starts] THEN [Waste of money/Overrun occurs]
   

Risk ID: R5
Paper ID: P01, P03, G01, G02
Risk Name: Cultural and Staff Resistance
Risk Description: Lack of support from employees or the wider organization due to fear of change or attachment to the old system.
Modernization Strategy: Holistic Modernization
Dimension: Organizational
Granularity Level: System-wide
Root Cause / Challenge: Cultural entrenchment in legacy processes and lack of clear communication about modernization benefits.
Trigger Event: Introduction of the modernization project or new software interfaces.
Impact / Consequences: Project sabotage, slow adoption, failure to realize business benefits.
Linked Risks: Funding withdrawal, Project cancellation, Time Overrun
Stakeholders: Employees, IT Staff, Management
Contextual Constraints: Trust in legacy system
Evidence Type: Statistical
Detection Source: Staff Surveys
Key Terminology (Tags): #CulturalResistance, #ChangeManagement, #StaffResistance, #TrustInProvenTech, #CustomerResistance
Standardized Risk Entry: Modernization efforts often face internal resistance due to a fear of expertise obsolescence and a cultural preference for "proven" reliable legacy systems.
Key Quote for Prompting: "Resistance to change from the organization... they fear that their expertise and professional experience... may become redundant."
Logic Pattern: IF [Shift to New Technology] AND [lack of communication] THEN [Cultural and Staff Resistance]
