QUESTION 30/35

A company replaces custom legacy software with a standard commercial product. Identify both the risks that may be reduced and the new risks that may be introduced.

ANSWER:
Replacing custom legacy software with a standard commercial product can reduce several risks, especially risks tied to bespoke maintenance and knowledge loss, but it can also introduce new vendor-related and integration-related risks.

### Risks that may be reduced
- **Application design risk** — lower risk because standard commercial products are typically more mature and tested. **[R1]**
- **Knowledge-availability risk** — reduced because the organization is less dependent on specialized in-house knowledge of custom code. **[R6]**
- **Vendor commitment risk** — reduced if the commercial product comes from an established vendor with ongoing support. **[R11]**
- **Product discontinuation risk** — reduced when using a standard product with an active market presence and support lifecycle. **[R12]**

### New risks that may be introduced
- **Customization risk** — the standard product may not fit all business requirements without modifications. **[R2]**
- **Interface risk** — integration with existing systems may become more complex. **[R3]**
- **Vendor dependency risk** — the organization may become dependent on the commercial vendor for future changes, support, and roadmap. **[R10]**
- **Vendor selection risk** — choosing the wrong commercial product or supplier can create strategic and operational issues. **[R5]**

### Overall relationship
This is an example of a tradeoff where modernization reduces some risks of bespoke legacy software but introduces dependency and fit/integration risks associated with packaged products. **[R7]**
