QUESTION 2/35
================================================================================
What causes R2 – Scarcity of Experts and Documentation, and what consequences can it have for a modernization project?

ANSWER:
R2 – **Scarcity of Experts and Documentation** is caused by **the aging workforce and a lack of continuous documentation updates during the system’s lifecycle**, especially when **key personnel retire or leave** [R2].

Its consequences for a modernization project can include:
- **Project delays**
- **Inability to understand business logic**
- **Increased risk of system failure during modernization** [R2]

It can also **trigger or worsen R1 – Incomplete or Erroneous Data Migration** and **R3 – Time Overrun** through related relationships [Rel338, Rel256].

Compare R5 – Cultural and Staff Resistance with R9 – User Resistance.

ANSWER:
R5 and R9 are both resistance-related modernization risks, but they differ in scope and origin:

- **R5 – Cultural and Staff Resistance** is an **organizational-level** risk. It describes **employees, IT staff, or the wider organization** resisting modernization because of fear of change, attachment to the old system, or concern that their expertise may become redundant. It can lead to **project sabotage, slow adoption, and failure to realize business benefits**.  
  - Relevant mitigation: **M20 Effective Change Management**, **M7 Staff Training and Developer Education**, **M9 Business Model & Culture Alignment**  
  - Relevant relationships: **Rel21** (R5 mitigated by M6), **Rel30** (R5 reduced by M7), **Rel61** (R5 prevented by M20), **Rel75** (R39 reduced by M27), **Rel245** (R5 reduced by M9)

- **R9 – User Resistance** is a more **user-interface / end-user adoption** risk. It occurs when users accustomed to efficient legacy character-based interfaces find a new GUI **slower or less intuitive**, leading to **lower satisfaction, initial productivity loss, and complaints during transition**.  
  - Relevant mitigation: **M7 Staff Training and Developer Education**, **M20 Effective Change Management**  
  - Relevant relationship: **Rel31** (R9 reduced by M7), **Rel62** (R9 reduced by M20)

### Main difference
- **R5** is about **organizational and staff culture resisting change**.
- **R9** is about **end users resisting a new interface or workflow**.

### Relationship between them
They are related because **R9 can trigger or worsen R5**:
- **Rel259**: Specific **user resistance (R9)** can cascade into broader **cultural and staff resistance (R5)**.

QUESTION 29/35
================================================================================
A Proof of Concept detects performance and API compatibility problems. Explain which documented risks were identified and how this information should influence the full modernization project.

ANSWER:
The Proof of Concept can reveal two documented risks:

- **R10 — Post-Modernization Performance Degradation**: the new environment may perform worse than the legacy system after cutover. A PoC can detect this early (**Rel22**, **Rel17**).
- **R20 — API and Data Incompatibility**: legacy interfaces may not work cleanly with the target cloud APIs or frameworks. A PoC can expose these compatibility issues before full migration (**Rel23**).

How this should influence the full modernization project:

- Use the PoC findings to **reassess the chosen modernization approach** and avoid committing to a path that will likely fail or require major rework (**R25**, **Rel20**, **Rel26**).
- Feed the results into **upfront planning and risk analysis** so the full project has realistic expectations, mitigation actions, and architecture adjustments (**R12**, **M8**, **Rel32**).
- If performance is the issue, incorporate **performance testing/monitoring** and architecture sizing before rollout (**M12**, **Rel46**, **Rel37**).
- If API compatibility is the issue, add **wrappers/adapters or other compatibility handling** before proceeding (**M23**, **Rel68**).

So, the PoC should not just “validate success”; it should be used as evidence to **refine the modernization design, update the roadmap, and reduce the risk of expensive full-scale failure** (**R10**, **R20**, **R25**, **R12**).
