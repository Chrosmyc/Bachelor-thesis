# Description
Break the system into manageable slices that are small enough to replace incrementally but meaningful enough to deliver business value.

# Prerequisites
Understanding of business functionality and system boundaries.

# Tools / Technologies
Thin slicing

# Trade-offs / Side Effects
Slices that are too small, too large, too low-value, or too high-risk create new risks.

# Effectiveness
The paper links smaller incremental changes with reduced risk and easier diagnosis/rectification of issues.

# Stakeholders
- Architects; developers; product owners; business stakeholders

# Contextual Constraints

- Strangler Fig modernization of large or complex systems.


# Standardized Mitigation Entry
Define thin, independent slices to make modernization changes smaller, more manageable, and easier to validate.

> 

# Logic Pattern
IF replacement scope is large THEN APPLY thin-slice decomposition.
