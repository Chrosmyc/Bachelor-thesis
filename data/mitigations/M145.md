# Description
Perform As-Is analysis, To-Be analysis, and Strategic Planning across business, application, data, and technology perspectives before moving into detailed planning and funding.

# Prerequisites
Existing information and target-state information can be collected from stakeholders and architecture sources.

# Tools / Technologies
Business architecture artifacts, application architecture artifacts, data architecture artifacts, technology architecture artifacts

# Trade-offs / Side Effects
Requires upfront time and cross-functional participation.

# Effectiveness
Produces a 360-degree view and a roadmap that becomes input for Planning and Funding.

# Stakeholders
- Business program areas
- architects
- technology teams
- agency leadership

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies during Due Diligence stage.


# Standardized Mitigation Entry
Due Diligence reduces modernization uncertainty by comparing current and target states and producing a strategic roadmap across business, application, data, and technology.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "As-Is and To-Be analysis"

# Logic Pattern
IF modernization uncertainty exists THEN APPLY Due Diligence across As-Is, To-Be, and Strategic Planning.
