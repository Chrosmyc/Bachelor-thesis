# Description
Run a planning session that assumes the modernization failed two years in the future, then ask why it failed, who felt the pain, and what went wrong.

# Prerequisites
Team participation; psychologically safe workshop environment; willingness to discuss failure scenarios.

# Tools / Technologies
Future postmortem workshop

# Trade-offs / Side Effects
Can surface uncomfortable organizational issues.

# Effectiveness
The paper says future postmortems expose risks not raised in traditional meetings and lead to a more honest, realistic roadmap.

# Stakeholders
- Modernization team; employees; team leads; CIO; change managers

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies where prior projects created cynicism or hidden resistance.


# Standardized Mitigation Entry
A future postmortem reduces emotional-debt risk by making hidden failure assumptions explicit before modernization execution.

> 

# Logic Pattern
IF teams distrust modernization or risks are hidden THEN APPLY a future postmortem workshop.
