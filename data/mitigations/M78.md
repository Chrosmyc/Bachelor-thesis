# Description
Decompose the system into smaller units responsible for delimited contexts and specific business capabilities.

# Prerequisites
Domain understanding and candidate microservice identification.

# Tools / Technologies
Domain-Driven Design (DDD)

# Trade-offs / Side Effects


# Effectiveness
The paper presents DDD as a prevalent approach and emphasizes low coupling, high cohesion, and clear scope.

# Stakeholders
- Software architects
- domain specialists
- development teams

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies during decomposition.


# Standardized Mitigation Entry
Designing services around bounded contexts, low coupling, high cohesion, and specific business capabilities mitigates wrong-granularity risk.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "each microservice must be responsible for a delimited context"

# Logic Pattern
IF microservice boundaries are being designed THEN APPLY low-coupling, high-cohesion, bounded-context criteria.
