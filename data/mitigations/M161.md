# Description
Avoid postponing modernization and modernize systems before the cost of inaction becomes larger than the investment. The paper frames modernization as the central response to profit, productivity, opportunity, customer, security, and maintenance-cost risks.

# Prerequisites
Decision to prioritize modernization; recognition that modernization delay is a strategic risk

# Tools / Technologies


# Trade-offs / Side Effects
Modernization investment may seem expensive; budget constraints and operational stability are mentioned as reasons for postponement

# Effectiveness
The paper claims that companies modernizing legacy systems in the past two years experienced 43% higher operational efficiency and a 31% increase in market share

# Stakeholders
- Executive leadership; COO; IT leadership; finance; business units

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies when modernization is being postponed or ignored despite business
- operational
- or security risks


# Standardized Mitigation Entry
On-time modernization mitigates the broad risk set created by delayed modernization by reducing reliance on outdated systems and enabling efficiency, agility, security, and growth.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "The cost of inaction is far greater than that."

# Logic Pattern
IF modernization delay creates strategic, operational, customer, or security risks THEN APPLY on-time system modernization.
