# Secondary Risk Created
Upfront analysis effort and cross-functional participation needs may increase planning effort.

# Applicability Conditions
Applies during Due Diligence when modernization roadmap decisions depend on current-state, target-state, and strategic planning information.

# Observed Outcome
Due Diligence produces a 360-degree view and a roadmap that becomes input for Planning and Funding.

# Reasoning Notes
Due Diligence does not replace detailed portfolio scoring, but it reduces roadmap misprioritization by grounding roadmap decisions in As-Is, To-Be, and Strategic Planning outputs across business, application, data, and technology perspectives.

# Standardized Relationship Entry
Due Diligence reduces roadmap misprioritization risk by grounding modernization sequencing in current-state analysis, target-state analysis, and strategic planning outputs.
