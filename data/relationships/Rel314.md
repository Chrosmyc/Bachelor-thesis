# Secondary Risk Created
Domain separation may be unnecessary where the system is large but functionally cohesive.

# Applicability Conditions
Suitable when the system is tightly integrated, cannot easily be split into distinct business domains, and must remain operational.

# Observed Outcome
The Strangler Pattern mitigates modernization transition risk by routing selected functionality to new components while the old system continues operating.

# Reasoning Notes
Strangler Pattern (M101) targets the root cause of Unanticipated Interdependency Derailment (R107): Unexpected interdependencies across system parts.

# Standardized Relationship Entry
IF Gradually replace parts of the legacy system by introducing a proxy or intermediary layer that intercepts requests and redirects them to new components THEN the risk of unanticipated interdependency derailment (R107) is prevented.
