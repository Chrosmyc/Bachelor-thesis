# Secondary Risk Created


# Applicability Conditions
Applies where the system can be broken into distinct domains while the legacy system remains operational during phased replacement.

# Observed Outcome
In the Alberta Child Care example, Strangler Pattern with SCS enabled old components to be replaced in phases and new features to be developed, tested, and deployed without affecting other components.

# Reasoning Notes
The combined approach addresses both transition risk and component impact risk by pairing phased replacement with independent modules.

# Standardized Relationship Entry
Strangler Pattern combined with SCS reduces non-isolated change-impact risk by replacing components in phases while organizing new work into independent modules.
