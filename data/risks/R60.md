# Description
A modernization project may be conducted with an inappropriate strategy or technique if the decision process does not align business, organizational, and technical perspectives.

# Root Cause
Modernization projects must align business, organizational, and technical perspectives and require decisions about whether to maintain, modernize, or replace.

# Trigger Event
The organization decides how to conduct modernization without a well-defined process, strategies, practices, and management recommendations.

# Impact
The selected modernization strategy may not fit the situation, causing ineffective modernization or escalation to replacement.

# Linked Risks

# Stakeholders

- Project managers; architects; business stakeholders; developers

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies after the organization decides modernization is needed and must choose strategies and techniques.


# Detection Source
Project Plan

# Standardized Risk Entry
If modernization decisions are made without a well-defined process that aligns business, organizational, and technical perspectives, the chosen strategy may be inappropriate for the context.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "the decision on how a modernization project should be conducted requires a well-defined process, including modernization strategies, good practices, and the recommendations for an effective management of the project towards modernization"

# Logic Pattern
IF modernization strategy selection lacks a defined process AND alignment across perspectives is required THEN the project may select an unsuitable modernization strategy.
