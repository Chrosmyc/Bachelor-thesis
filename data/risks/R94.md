# Description
Existing skills and experience may become scarce due to ageing and staff changing jobs, causing knowledge erosion during modernization.

# Root Cause
Critical knowledge resides in people and documentation is incomplete or outdated.

# Trigger Event
Migration proceeds without harvesting and preserving knowledge from technical staff.

# Impact
Loss of system understanding, incomplete inventories, weaker migration decisions.

# Linked Risks
- R94; R95; R100

# Stakeholders

- Technical staff; migration team; maintenance team; program management committee

# Contextual Constraints

- Long-lived legacy subsystems with scarce skills and incomplete documentation.


# Detection Source
Interview; Documentation; Workshops

# Standardized Risk Entry
Knowledge erosion can undermine modernization when scarce technical expertise is not captured and preserved before or during migration.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "such knowledge and skills become scarce resulting in knowledge erosion due to factors such as ageing, and staff changing jobs"

# Logic Pattern
IF critical system knowledge resides in staff AND knowledge is not harvested THEN knowledge erosion may harm migration decisions.
