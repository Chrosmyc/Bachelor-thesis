# Description
Modernization programs may fail because organizations lack structured, repeatable operating routines for planning, executing, validating, and handing off complex technology change.

# Root Cause
Absence of institutionalized governance infrastructure and repeatable operating model.

# Trigger Event
Initiating large-scale modernization without controlled governance routines, decision gates, validation checkpoints, and handoff practices.

# Impact
Costly modernization failure, uncontrolled execution, waste, schedule variance, and unsafe propagation of failure modes into production.

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies to complex enterprise modernization across migrations
- data platforms
- AI automation
- and mission-critical systems.


# Detection Source
Project Plan / Architecture Diagram / Operational Data

# Standardized Risk Entry
Large-scale modernization can fail when governance routines for planning, execution, validation, and handoff are not institutionalized. EMRGF treats this as a primary modernization risk rather than an engineering-capability problem.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "These are not engineering failures. They are governance failures — the predictable consequences of fragmented architecture decisions, absent migration standards, undisciplined change control, and the absence of repeatable quality gates that catch failure modes before they propagate into production."

# Logic Pattern
IF modernization is executed without structured governance routines AND complex technology change spans multiple teams THEN governance deficit may cause modernization failure.
