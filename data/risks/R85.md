# Description
Premature technical decisions during migration may decompose the system too aggressively, favoring autonomy while neglecting business impact.

# Root Cause
Transition treated as a mainly technical decision rather than aligned with business objectives.

# Trigger Event
Technical decomposition decisions are made before business impact is sufficiently considered.

# Impact
Negative business impact and potentially unsuitable service boundaries.

# Linked Risks
- R86

# Stakeholders

- Software architects
- development teams
- domain specialists
- business stakeholders

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies during modernization from legacy systems to MSA.


# Detection Source
Architecture Diagram / Project Plan / Interview

# Standardized Risk Entry
Premature technical decisions in microservice migration can cause overly aggressive decomposition that increases autonomy but harms business fit.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "precipitated technical decisions may lead to very aggressive decomposition ... without considering the business impact"

# Logic Pattern
IF migration decisions are made primarily on technical criteria AND business impact is not considered THEN overly aggressive decomposition may occur.
