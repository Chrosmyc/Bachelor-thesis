# Description
The modernization business case may underestimate probable risks and fail to define corrective actions, weakening the factual basis for funding and decision-making.

# Root Cause
CIOs may not fully consider all negative implications and may not involve all impacted business units and IT stakeholders.

# Trigger Event
A modernization business case is prepared without a thorough cross-stakeholder risk assessment.

# Impact
Risks remain unquantified, corrective actions remain undefined, the proposal is less persuasive, and modernization funding may be delayed or rejected.

# Linked Risks
- R150

# Stakeholders

- CIO; IT team; impacted business unit stakeholders; CEO; CFO

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies to midsize enterprises seeking funding for legacy modernization.


# Detection Source
Project Plan / Interview / Application strategy assessment

# Standardized Risk Entry
If modernization risk identification is incomplete, the business case may fail to quantify exposure and define corrective actions, reducing the chance of funding approval.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> [paraphrased] The business case should identify and quantify probable risks and corrective actions.

# Logic Pattern
IF modernization funding is requested AND risk assessment is incomplete THEN modernization risks may remain under-quantified and under-mitigated.
