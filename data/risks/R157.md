# Description
Delaying modernization can sustain system delays, errors, or disjointed customer experiences that cause customers to leave.

# Root Cause
Outdated systems cannot keep pace with evolving customer expectations and create a disjointed experience

# Trigger Event
Customers encounter delays or errors because outdated systems remain in use

# Impact
Customer defection, reduced trust, lower loyalty, and loss of valuable customers

# Linked Risks
- R155; R156

# Stakeholders

- Customers; customer support; sales; marketing; executive leadership; IT operations

# Contextual Constraints

- Customer-facing systems or processes where outdated technology can create delays
- errors
- or inconsistent experiences


# Detection Source
Operational Data

# Standardized Risk Entry
If modernization is delayed and customer-facing systems create delays or errors, customers may defect and brand trust may decline.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "If your systems can’t keep pace with customer demands, you risk alienating your most valuable asset: your customers."

# Logic Pattern
IF modernization is delayed AND customers experience system-related delays or errors THEN customer exit and trust loss may occur.
