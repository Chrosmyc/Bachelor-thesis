# Description
Organizations may return to older operational behaviors after investing in digital systems, such as printed reports, manual approvals, spreadsheet tracking, partially ignored workflows, and legacy-style decision structures.

# Root Cause
Organizational culture and operational behavior change slower than technology

# Trigger Event
Deployment of automation, dashboards, digital workflows, analytics systems, or AI-driven operations

# Impact
Limited operational value from modernization investments; reduced effectiveness of digital workflows; persistence of legacy thinking

# Linked Risks
- R34
- R35
- R39

# Stakeholders

- Operational teams; leadership; modernization-oriented personnel

# Contextual Constraints

- Enterprises that purchase modern technologies while operational culture remains resistant to change


# Detection Source
Operational Data / Project Plan / Interview

# Standardized Risk Entry
After technology modernization, operational teams may revert to manual processes, causing digital workflows and dashboards to be underused and limiting modernization value.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "Despite heavy investment in automation, organizations slowly return to older operational behaviors."

# Logic Pattern
IF modern digital workflows are deployed AND operational culture remains attached to familiar manual processes THEN the organization may revert to manual operations.
