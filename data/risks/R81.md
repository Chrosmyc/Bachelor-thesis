# Description
Modernization with microservices may fail or waste resources if feasibility, benefits, duration, constraints, and available resources are not analyzed before the migration starts.

# Root Cause
Modernization is long, risky, and affects all company perspectives; driving forces and resources may be unclear or conflicting.

# Trigger Event
A company starts microservice modernization without analyzing driving forces, business goals, feasibility, and resources.

# Impact
Unsuccessful modernization; waste of human, financial, and material resources; negative impact across technical, operational, and organizational perspectives.

# Linked Risks
- R82
- R83
- R84

# Stakeholders

- Managers; engineers; developers; customers

# Contextual Constraints

- Applies when microservice modernization is being considered as a feasible solution for a monolithic legacy system.


# Detection Source
Project Plan / Interview

# Standardized Risk Entry
If modernization with microservices starts without clear feasibility and driving-force analysis, the project risks becoming unsuccessful and wasting resources.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> modernization of legacy systems is a long and risky process; Analyze the driving forces is paramount to avoid unsuccessful modernization and waste of resources

# Logic Pattern
IF driving forces, feasibility, resources, and constraints are unclear AND modernization is initiated THEN risk of unsuccessful modernization and wasted resources.
