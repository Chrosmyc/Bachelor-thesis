# Description
Executive leadership may support and fund modernization while operational levels resist or fail to adopt the required changes, creating a gap between strategic objectives and daily practice.

# Root Cause
Leadership may not fully recognize internal resistance, workforce concerns, or cultural barriers

# Trigger Event
Executive investment in modernization combined with operational-level resistance

# Impact
Delayed implementation, low adoption rates, reduced operational effectiveness, misdiagnosis of failure as technology limitation

# Linked Risks
- R34
- R35
- R36
- R39

# Stakeholders

- Executive leadership; senior decision-makers; operational teams; technical teams

# Contextual Constraints

- Enterprise environments where modernization is driven from the executive level but adoption depends on operational teams


# Detection Source
Project Plan / Interview / Operational Data

# Standardized Risk Entry
Modernization can fail when leadership objectives and investments are not matched by operational-level adoption, causing delayed implementation and reduced effectiveness.

# Key Quote for Prompting
> "This creates a disconnect between strategic leadership objectives and operational reality."

# Logic Pattern
IF leadership invests in modernization AND operational teams continue favoring familiar manual processes THEN strategic objectives may disconnect from operational reality.
